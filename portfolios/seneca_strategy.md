# The Seneca Strategy

> *"It is not that we have a short time to live, but that we waste a lot of it. Life is long enough, and a sufficiently generous amount has been given to us for the highest achievements if it were all well invested."*
> — **Seneca**, On the Shortness of Life

---

## Philosophy

The crowd follows emotion. We follow reason.

Seneca taught **premeditatio malorum** — the premeditation of evils. By mentally rehearsing worst-case scenarios, we:

1. **Prepare** for what others ignore
2. **Profit** when the obvious becomes reality
3. **Remain calm** while others panic

---

## The Common Sense Checklist

Before placing a contrarian bet, ask:

| Question | If YES → Bet Against Crowd |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Is everyone agreeing? | Consensus is usually wrong at extremes |
| Does history suggest otherwise? | Markets have short memories |
| Are fundamentals being ignored? | Price ≠ Value |
| Is it "priced in" but hasn't happened? | Markets front-run, then forget |
| Would your grandmother understand the risk? | Common sense beats complexity |

Edge = (Our Probability × Payout) - 1

## Seneca's Rules

1. **Bet only what you can lose entirely**
   > *"It is not the man who has too little, but the man who craves more, that is poor."*

2. **The crowd is usually wrong at extremes**
   > *"Religion is regarded by the common people as true, by the wise as false, and by rulers as useful."*

3. **Prepare for the worst, profit from the preparation**
   > *"The whole future lies in uncertainty: live immediately."*

4. **Don't fight the trend — wait for the turn**
   > *"Luck is what happens when preparation meets opportunity."*

5. **Accept losses as tuition**
   > *"Difficulties strengthen the mind, as labor does the body."*

## The Grandmother Test

Before any bet, ask: **"Would Grandma understand this risk?"**

| Market | Grandmother Logic |
|--------|-------------------|
| Recession | "Jobs are getting harder to find. Prices are still high. Something's wrong." |
| Inflation | "Everything costs more. Gas, groceries, rent. They say it's fixed but I don't see it." |
| Shutdown | "Those politicians can never agree on anything." |
| Market crash | "Stocks can't go up forever. What goes up must come down." |

If Grandma gets it, and the market doesn't price it, **bet with Grandma.**
